  • The data types shown in the bubble and pie chart are both attributes and positions. Attributes refers to the different "slices" of pie chart. Their color would classify as a categorical attribute, while their area is a quantitative attribute. Lastly, the positon of the pies/bubbles is also a datum since it's relevant to the information being communicated. For the marks and channels of this visual, we identified two marks: points for the "bubble" aspect of the visual, and area for the "pie" aspect of the visual. For the channels, we saw three possible channel types in the visual: One, the different colors or categories of the pie chart, Two, the position of the pies/bubbles on the X and Y axis, and 3, the area of a pie/bubble on the X and Y axis. This differs from the area mark, as the mark refers to the area of each category inside each pie/bubble, while the channel refers to the whole area of a pie/bubble, especially in relativity to the other data points.
